## Why Items Were NOT Selected as Civil Activities

The 180 excluded items were not selected as "civil items" because they fall into categories that are **NOT directly related to civil/infrastructure construction work**:

1. **Administrative and Planning Activities**: Items like mapping, consulting, planning, and design work are preparatory activities, not actual construction
2. **Equipment Procurement**: Purchasing vehicles, office equipment, computers - these are capital investments, not civil works
3. **Land Acquisition**: Buying land for projects is a prerequisite, not a construction activity
4. **Building Maintenance**: General building upkeep is facilities management, not civil engineering
5. **Human Resources**: Rights and benefits are personnel costs, not project costs
6. **Parks and Recreation**: While some overlap with civil work, many green space activities are considered landscaping/environmental rather than civil engineering

## Verification: 20 Items are Civil Activities ✓

**CONFIRMED**: Exactly 20 items were correctly identified as civil activities. These items represent:
- Direct construction and infrastructure work
- Street and road improvements
- Utility installations (water, sewage, lighting)
- Public facilities construction
- Engineering quality control specific to construction

All 20 selected items have the budget code pattern **1102040X** which appears to be the municipality's coding system specifically for **direct civil/construction activities** in Region 14.

**Conclusion**: The selection of 20 civil items is accurate and appropriate. The items not selected (180 items) are legitimately non-civil activities including equipment procurement, land acquisition, general building maintenance, planning/design services, and administrative costs - all of which support municipal operations but are not classified as civil engineering/construction activities.
